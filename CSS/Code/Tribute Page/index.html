Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Legendary Indian Cricketer

Biography

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, commonly known as MS Dhoni, is a former Indian international cricketer and captain. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, Jharkhand, Dhoni made his debut for the Indian cricket team in December 2004.

Renowned for his calm demeanor and remarkable leadership skills, Dhoni captained the Indian cricket team in all formats. Under his captaincy, India won numerous prestigious tournaments, including the 2007 ICC World Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asia Cups, the 2011 ICC Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.

Throughout his illustrious career, Dhoni earned the nickname "Captain Cool" for his ability to remain composed under pressure. He retired from international cricket in August 2020, leaving behind a legacy that will be cherished by cricket enthusiasts worldwide.

Mahendra Singh Dhoni's contributions to Indian cricket are unparalleled, and his impact on the sport will be remembered for generations to come.
